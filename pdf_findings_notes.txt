Source notes from uploaded PDFs for Rights Require Routes / Custody Crisis story form

1) From /home/ubuntu/upload/RightsRequireRoutes_ShareYourStory.pdf
- Tone is trust-based and listening-centered.
- Key promises include: every story is read carefully; every story contributes to deeper understanding; if the person chooses, the story may become part of future writing or research; the person remains in control of how the story is used; nothing is ever published without permission.
- Form fields shown include country, state/province/region, case type, case status, self-represented, participation barriers, accommodation/support request, what happened, what would you tell yourself, what advice would you give someone starting, a moment that captures the feeling of it, and story-use permission options.

2) From /home/ubuntu/upload/CustodyCrisis—Stories.Research.Change_.pdf
- Project framing: research and advocacy project collecting stories to document patterns, not provide legal advice or fix individual cases.
- Includes crisis resources near the top.
- Participation barriers list is more detailed than current page and includes:
  attention/focus difficulties; memory or recall difficulties; anxiety/panic/emotional overwhelm; difficulty processing information quickly; communication barriers; executive function barriers; mobility or physical access barriers; sensory barriers; chronic illness or pain; trauma or PTSD; other barrier; no specific barriers, I was just not heard.
- Includes accommodation request section with two required selects:
  a) Did you ask for any accommodation or support?
  b) What happened?
- Includes optional reflective textareas:
  a) If you could go back in time, what would you tell yourself?
  b) What advice would you give to someone just starting this process?
  c) A moment, a quote, a journal entry, or a detail that captures what this was like.
- Includes story-use permission options (radio buttons), not just a single checkbox:
  a) Research only: Use my story for analysis, but don't quote or identify me.
  b) Anonymous quote: You can use a quote, but with no identifying details.
  c) First name or initials: You can identify me by first name or initials.
  d) Full name: You can use my full name.
