# Rights Require Routes: Research Charter

**Project:** Rights Require Routes (CustodyCrisis.org)
**Principal Investigator:** Katie Copeland, Texas Attorney & Advocate
**Effective Date:** June 2026

---

## 1. Project Purpose
The family court system frequently fails to accommodate the cognitive, emotional, and physical realities of the people navigating it. **Rights Require Routes** exists to document these invisible barriers. By collecting lived experiences from pro se litigants and protective parents, this project aims to translate individual isolation into systemic visibility, research, and advocacy.

## 2. The Agreement with Participants
When you share your story with Rights Require Routes, this is our commitment to you:

* **Your story is yours.** You decide how it is used. We will never publish your name or identifying details unless you explicitly select the "Full name" or "First name/initials" permission level.
* **We listen for patterns, not judgment.** We are looking for systemic failures—where the court process itself created barriers related to memory, processing, anxiety, trauma, or physical access.
* **We cannot fix your individual case.** This project is not a law firm and cannot provide legal advice, representation, or crisis intervention. 
* **You can change your mind.** If you decide later that you want your story removed from the database, you can email us and it will be deleted.

## 3. How the Data Will Be Used
The stories collected will be synthesized to identify systemic patterns in family court access. The data may be used for:
* **Academic & Legal Research:** Identifying how ADA Title II and basic access principles are failing in family courts.
* **Public Advocacy:** Creating reports, articles, or presentations to educate judges, lawyers, and policymakers.
* **Community Validation:** Sharing aggregated findings back with the community so participants know they are not alone.

## 4. Shared Power & Transparency
This project operates on the principle that lived experience is expertise. 
* Findings will be shared in plain language, accessible to the community, not just locked behind academic paywalls.
* The project will continually refine its focus based on what the community reveals in the data.

## 5. Security & Privacy
Data is stored in a secure, private Notion database accessible only to the Principal Investigator. We practice data minimization—we only ask for what we need to understand the systemic issue. See our full Privacy Policy for technical details.
