# Rights Require Routes: Trauma-Informed Intake & Escalation Protocol

**Purpose:** To define how Rights Require Routes (a project of CustodyCrisis.org) safely handles story submissions, particularly when submissions contain disclosures of crisis, ongoing abuse, or legally sensitive information.

**Scope:** This protocol applies to all data collected via the Rights Require Routes story intake form.

---

## 1. Core Principles of Intake
* **Do No Harm:** The intake process must not create new risks for participants.
* **Non-Intervention:** Rights Require Routes is a research and advocacy project, not a crisis response or legal service. We do not intervene in individual cases.
* **Participant Autonomy:** Participants own their stories. They may request deletion of their data at any time.

## 2. Crisis & Safety Escalation Protocol
Because family court experiences inherently involve trauma, submissions may occasionally indicate immediate danger. 

**Pre-Submission Prevention:**
* The intake form permanently displays crisis resources (988 Lifeline, National DV Hotline) at the top of the page.
* The consent language explicitly states that the form is not monitored 24/7 and is not a channel for emergency help.

**Post-Submission Review:**
When reviewing submissions, if a story indicates **imminent, life-threatening danger to self or others** (e.g., active suicide plan, active threat of lethal violence):
1. **Do not attempt direct clinical intervention.** Rights Require Routes staff are not emergency responders.
2. **Do not contact opposing parties or courts.** 
3. **Action:** If contact information is provided, send a standardized, compassionate email providing national crisis resources and clarifying the limits of the project's capacity to assist. 
4. **Data Handling:** Redact identifying information from the database while preserving the thematic data for research, unless deletion is requested.

## 3. Handling Legal & Abuse Disclosures
Submissions will frequently detail allegations of abuse, systemic failure, and ongoing litigation.

* **No Attorney-Client Relationship:** The form explicitly waives the creation of an attorney-client relationship. No legal advice will be provided in response to submissions.
* **Duty to Report:** As an independent research project, Rights Require Routes is not a mandated reporter. However, to protect participants, the project does not collect identifying information of minors. 
* **Subpoena Risk:** To mitigate the risk of data being weaponized in ongoing family court litigation, the project relies on the principle of data minimization. We only collect what is voluntarily provided, and we honor the participant's chosen privacy level (e.g., "Research Only").

## 4. Data Deletion & Revocation of Consent
Participants may experience a change in their legal strategy or emotional readiness.
* If a participant requests deletion of their story, the record will be permanently deleted from the Notion database within 72 hours.
* Deletion requests can be sent to katie@custodycrisis.org.

## 5. Researcher Wellbeing
Reviewing family court trauma at scale carries a high risk of vicarious trauma.
* Reviews should be batched and time-boxed.
* The researcher (Katie Copeland) will utilize nervous-system regulation practices and maintain firm boundaries around reading times (e.g., not immediately before sleep).
